# Complex-composite PIT packages

## Hong Kong salaries tax

The Hong Kong package calculates salaries tax for years of assessment 2023/24, 2024/25 and 2025/26 by comparing two statutory liabilities and selecting the lower amount.

## Input contract

The caller supplies:

- confirmation that Hong Kong salaries tax applies;
- net income after deductions but before personal allowances;
- net chargeable income after personal allowances.

Net chargeable income must not exceed net income. TaxCraft does not derive deductions or allowances and does not request names, marital status, spouse details, identity numbers or tax documents.

## Progressive-rate candidate

Tax is calculated on net chargeable income:

- 2% on the first HKD 50,000;
- 6% on the next HKD 50,000;
- 10% on the next HKD 50,000;
- 14% on the next HKD 50,000;
- 17% on the remainder.

## Standard-rate candidate

For 2023/24, net income is taxed at 15%.

From 2024/25:

- the first HKD 5 million of net income is taxed at 15%;
- the remainder is taxed at 16%.

## Lower-of comparison

The package calculates both candidates and selects the lower pre-reduction liability. The output reports:

- progressive-rate tax;
- standard-rate tax;
- selected tax before reduction;
- final-tax reduction available and applied;
- net salaries tax.

The assumptions identify which schedule was selected, while the calculation lines reconcile only the selected schedule to net tax.

## Final-tax reductions

The package applies the legislated reduction non-refundably:

- up to HKD 3,000 for 2023/24;
- up to HKD 1,500 for 2024/25;
- up to HKD 3,000 for 2025/26.

## Exclusions

The package excludes:

- deduction, allowance and net-chargeable-income derivation;
- married-person and joint-assessment decisions;
- personal assessment, property tax and profits tax;
- provisional tax, withholding, instalments and payment reconciliation;
- residence, source, employment-income classification and filing obligations.

## Sources

The maintained models use official Hong Kong Inland Revenue Department rate examples, the statutory lower-of description, two-tier standard-rate measures and annual final-tax reduction measures.
