# Complex-composite PIT packages — wave 2

## Scope

Wave 2 adds the United States tax year 2026 federal ordinary-income rate schedules.

The package accepts only caller-confirmed federal taxable income and a caller-confirmed filing status. It does not derive adjusted gross income, deductions, filing eligibility or any identity-bearing fact.

## Supported calculation

The executable model supports:

- single;
- married filing jointly;
- married filing separately;
- head of household;
- the 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35% and 37% federal ordinary-income bands for tax year 2026;
- whole-dollar flooring of federal taxable income before the rate schedule is applied;
- deterministic cent-denominated results and band-level source attribution.

## Official sources

The parameters are taken from Internal Revenue Service Revenue Procedure 2025-32, section 4.01, with the IRS tax-year inflation-adjustment index retained as the maintenance reference.

## Exclusions

The package does not calculate or determine:

- gross income, adjusted gross income, deductions or taxable income;
- filing-status, dependent, residency, citizenship or filing eligibility;
- qualified-dividend, capital-gain or other preferential-rate schedules;
- alternative minimum tax, net investment income tax or additional Medicare tax;
- tax credits, self-employment tax, payroll tax, withholding or prior payments;
- state, District of Columbia, territorial or local income taxes;
- estate, trust, expatriation, gift or estate taxes;
- penalties, interest, refunds, treaty positions or return reconciliation.

## Acceptance boundary

Implementation includes the country package, closed input schema, catalogue and API registration, boundary fixtures, privacy tests and official-source attribution.

Repository acceptance requires the exact branch head to pass the full repository and hardened-container workflow. Deployment and live acceptance remain separate states and are not claimed by this work package.
